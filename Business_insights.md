## Business Insights – Supermarket Sales Analysis

This document summarizes key insights derived from SQL analysis of the supermarket sales dataset.

---

1. Overall Revenue Performance

Observation

The dataset contains all supermarket transactions and their corresponding sales values.

Insight

The dataset provides a complete view of sales transactions across branches, customers, and product categories, allowing identification of key revenue drivers.

Action

Businesses can use this data to monitor overall sales performance and track growth trends over time.

---

2. Revenue by Branch

Observation

Revenue varies across different branches.

Insight

Some branches contribute higher revenue compared to others, suggesting differences in customer demand, location advantage, or store performance.

Action

Analyze operational strategies of high-performing branches and replicate them in lower-performing locations.

---

3. Product Line Performance

Observation

Sales revenue is distributed across multiple product categories.

Insight

Certain product lines generate higher sales than others, indicating stronger customer demand in those categories.

Action

Focus inventory management and promotional campaigns on top-performing product lines.

---

4. Customer Type Revenue

Observation

- Member revenue: 164K
- Normal customer revenue: 158K

Insight

Member customers generate slightly higher revenue than normal customers, suggesting that loyalty programs encourage higher spending.

Action

Strengthen membership programs to increase repeat purchases and long-term customer value.

---

5. Sales by Time of Day

Observation

- Afternoon revenue: 172K
- Evening revenue: 88K
- Morning revenue: 61K

Insight

Sales peak during the afternoon, indicating the highest customer activity during midday hours.

Action

Increase staffing and ensure product availability during peak afternoon sales periods.

---

6. Payment Method Preference

Observation

- E-wallet transactions: 345
- Cash transactions: 344
- Credit card transactions: 311

Insight

Customers frequently use both e-wallet and cash payments, while credit card usage is comparatively lower.

Action

Encourage digital payments through offers or cashback incentives to improve transaction efficiency.

---

Final Business Summary

The SQL analysis reveals several important business patterns:

- Revenue performance varies across branches.
- Certain product categories drive the majority of sales.
- Loyalty members contribute slightly higher revenue.
- Afternoon hours represent peak shopping activity.
- Digital payment methods are widely adopted by customers.

These insights can help businesses optimize store operations, improve marketing strategies, and enhance customer experience.
